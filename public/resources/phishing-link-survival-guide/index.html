  • If the link took you to a website, DO NOT enter passwords, credit card numbers, Social Security numbers, or ANY personal information.
  • Close the browser or app immediately.
  • Do not click "Yes," "No," or any buttons on pop-up windows. Just close the window.
Step 2 â€” Stop Any Downloads
  • Check your browser to see if any files are currently downloading. Cancel them.
  • Look in your "Downloads" folder. If you see a file you didn't ask for (like a .zip or .exe), DO NOT OPEN IT. Delete it immediately.
Step 3 â€” Disconnect from the Internet Immediately
  • WiFi: Turn off WiFi on your device OR unplug your home WiFi router from the wall.
  • Wired Connection: Unplug the ethernet cable from your computer.
  • Why? This cuts the connection to the scammer and prevents malware from sending your data to criminals.

Step 4 â€” Back Up Important Files

If you have an external hard drive or USB stick, copy your most essential documents and photos to it now.

  • Focus on irreplaceable items like family photos or financial records.
  • Warning: Do NOT back up any files that were just downloaded from the suspicious link.
Step 5 â€” Scan for Malware

Run a complete scan using your antivirus software. Do this while still disconnected from the internet if possible.

  • If your scan finds a threat, let the software delete or quarantine it.
  • If you aren't sure how to do this, consider taking your device to a professional repair shop.
  • Tip: If you have a second computer, you can download a free tool like Malwarebytes, put it on a USB drive, and install it on the affected computer.

Step 6 â€” Change ALL Your Passwords

Using a different device (like a phone or tablet not connected to your home WiFi), change your passwords.

  • Priority: Email accounts, Online Banking, and Retirement accounts.
  • Create strong, unique passwords for every account.
  • Never use the same password for two different accounts.
Step 7 â€” Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Turn on "Two-Factor Authentication" for your email and bank accounts.

  • This adds a second lock to your door. Even if a scammer has your password, they can't get in without the code sent to your phone.
Step 8 â€” Contact Your Bank and Credit Card Companies

Call the fraud department of your financial institutions.

  • Tell them: "I may have clicked on a phishing link and want to protect my account."
  • Ask them to watch for suspicious charges.
  • Consider asking for a temporary freeze on your cards.
