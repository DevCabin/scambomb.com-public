Recognizing the scam is half the battle. Watch out for these five tricks:

1. Fake Package Delivery

What it is: Scammers pretend to be USPS, FedEx, or Amazon.

The Message:

"USPS: Your package US-9283 is on hold due to address issue. Click here to reschedule delivery."

The Trap: The link takes you to a fake website that steals your credit card info or installs a virus.

2. Phony Job Offers / "Task Scams"

What it is: Offers for easy, high-paying work from home.

The Message:

"Earn $500/day rating simple apps! No experience needed. Reply YES to start earning."

The Trap: They ask you to send money to "unlock" your earnings or buy equipment.

3. Fake Bank Alerts

What it is: A terrifying message claiming your money is being stolen.

The Message:

"CHASE BANK ALERT: Suspicious charge of $2,847 at Apple Store. Reply YES or NO."

The Trap: If you reply, they call pretending to be the "Fraud Department" and trick you into transferring money to "safe" accounts â€” which are theirs.

4. Bogus Toll Notices

What it is: Claims you owe a small toll fee.

The Message:

"Toll Services: You have an unpaid toll of $3.45. Pay now to avoid $50 late fee penalties."

The Trap: They want your credit card number and Social Security Number.

5. "Wrong Number" Investment Scams

What it is: A friendly text meant for "someone else."

The Message:

"Hey Amanda, are we still on for dinner tonight?"

The Trap: When you reply "wrong number," they start a friendly conversation. Over weeks, they build a fake friendship â€” then convince you to invest in fake cryptocurrency schemes.

Red Flags Checklist

Print this page and keep it by your phone.

  • Creates urgency ("Act now!" "Immediate action required!")
  • Asks for payment via unusual methods (gift cards, crypto, wire transfer)
  • Claims to be from government/bank but uses a normal cell phone number
  • Contains links asking you to click "immediately"
  • Threatens consequences (account closure, arrest, penalties)
  • Offers prizes or refunds that seem too good to be true
  • Asks for sensitive info (SSN, passwords, account numbers)
  • Contains spelling or grammar errors
  • Uses generic greetings ("Dear Customer" instead of your name)
