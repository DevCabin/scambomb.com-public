Why Scams Work

Scams are not successful because people are stupid.

They are successful because scammers study human behavior.

  • Use same techniques as marketing and advertising
  • Trigger emotional reactions before we can think
  • Count on us being busy, distracted, or reacting quickly
  • ANYONE can fall for a scam under the right circumstances

The Five Signals of a Scam

Most scams follow a recognizable pattern. Once you know the signals, they become much easier to identify.

1. Urgency

"Act now", "Your account will be closed", "Your grandchild is in trouble."

2. Authority

Pretending to be a bank, the IRS, Amazon, Microsoft, or law enforcement.

3. Emotion

Fear, concern, excitement, greed, or love.

4. Unusual Payment

Gift cards, wire transfers, crypto, or payment apps.

5. Secrecy

"Don't tell anyone", "Keep this confidential."

Remember

Scams almost always contain at least two of these signals, often three or four.

The Golden Rule

If a message creates urgency
→ Slow down ←

Scams depend on speed.
Protection comes from pausing and verifying.

The Grandparent Scam

Traditional Version

  • Someone calls pretending to be a grandchild in trouble
  • Claims car accident, arrested, or stranded
  • Urgently needs money immediately
  • Goal: Create panic so person doesn't verify the story

This scam has existed for years. But now it has evolved with AI technology.

The New Version: AI Voice Cloning

ABC7 Investigation: AI Voice Scam

Imagine This...

How many of you have grandchildren or younger family members?

"Now imagine getting a phone call that sounds like one of them... saying they're in trouble."

That emotional reaction is exactly what scammers are counting on.

How the AI Voice Scam Works

1

Harvest

Scammers find 3-30 seconds of voice from social media, voicemail, YouTube, or TikTok

2

Clone

AI software learns tone, pitch, and patterns to recreate the voice

3

Attack

Call victim with cloned voice claiming emergency, needing money immediately

Scammers almost always tell victims: "Don't tell anyone — it'll make things worse." This is the #1 manipulation tactic.

Why This Scam Works

This scam succeeds because it combines three powerful triggers:

1. Familiar Voice

Sounds exactly like your loved one

2. Urgent Emergency

Creates immediate stress response

3. Pressure to Act

No time to think or verify

When these three things happen together, people often react emotionally
before they have time to stop and verify.

The Simple Defense

Hang Up and Verify

  1. Hang up the phone
  2. Call the family member directly using a known number (not one provided by the caller)
  3. Confirm the emergency with other family members if possible
  4. Never send money under pressure without verification

Remember: AI may change how scams sound, but the defense remains the same.
