Question 1 of 5
Among adults age 50 and older,
how many have used AI in some form?
AAbout 1 in 3  (33%)
BMore than half  (55%)
CAbout 3 in 4  (77%)
Answer — Question 1
55%
✓ More than half

In a survey of nearly 3,000 Americans over 50, 55% reported using some type of AI technology — either a voice assistant like Alexa, or a text chatbot like ChatGPT.

AI is already in more lives than most people realize.

Question 2 of 5
Among older adults who use AI —
which type do they use more?
AText chatbots like ChatGPT — twice as popular
BVoice assistants like Alexa — twice as popular
CAbout equal — people use both the same amount
Answer — Question 2
Voice
Assistants
✓ And it's not close

50% used a voice assistant in the past year. Only 25% used a text chatbot — half as many.

Talking to technology feels natural. Typing to it is still a newer habit for many.

Question 3 of 5
Of older adults using AI-powered home security cameras and doorbells,
how many said they felt safer?
AAbout 3 in 4  (72%)
BAbout 5 in 6  (84%)
CNearly all of them  (96%)
Answer — Question 3
96%
✓ Nearly all of them

Of older adults using AI-powered home security, a remarkable 96% said they felt safer — one of the highest satisfaction rates in the entire study.

When AI is practical and tangible, people embrace it quickly.

Question 4 of 5
When asked whether they trust AI-generated content,
what did older adults say?
AMost trust it — about 3 in 4 said yes
BThey're split roughly down the middle
CMost don't — fewer than 1 in 3 said yes
Answer — Question 4
Right Down
the Middle
✓ 54% trust it  /  46% do not

54% said they trust AI-generated content. 46% said they do not. And only half felt confident they could spot when AI content was wrong.

And it's getting more difficult to tell every day.

Question 5 of 5
What share of older adults said they want to know when content was made by AI?
AAbout 6 in 10  (60%)
BAbout 3 in 4  (75%)
C9 in 10  (90%)
Answer — Question 5
90%
✓ 9 in 10 — overwhelming consensus

9 in 10 older adults want to know when content was generated by AI. On top of that, nearly 80% said they want to learn more about AI risks — where it can go wrong and what to do about it.

Another good use of AI tools — to detect other AI tools!
