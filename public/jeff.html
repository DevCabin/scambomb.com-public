Session Goals

The Scam Pattern We’re Studying

  • Unexpected recruiter outreach (text/email) from a “known” company.
  • Fast trust-building + text-only interview flow.
  • High pay for simple remote work.
  • “We’ll send you a check for equipment” pivot.

Five Red Flags (Memorize These)

1) Unsolicited Contact

Out-of-the-blue offers are high risk.

2) Text-Only Interview

Real hiring rarely happens only by text.

3) Too-Good Pay

Big pay for low-complexity tasks is a lure.

4) Urgency + Fast Offer

Scammers compress your decision window.

5) Check for Supplies

Classic fake-check setup.

Jeff’s Safety Rule

If money, identity info, or urgency is involved: pause, verify, and escalate before any action.

  • Never trust contact details provided by the sender.
  • Verify through official company website and known channels.
  • Ask: “What’s the downside if this is fake?”

Do This Immediately If Exposed

  • Stop communication and save screenshots/messages.
  • If money moved, contact bank/payment provider immediately.
  • If personal info shared, treat as identity-risk event now.
  • Report to FTC + IC3 and platform/company impersonated.

Role-Play Drill

Prompt A

“Congratulations, you’re hired. We’ll mail a check for your office setup.”

Response Script

“I only continue after independent verification from your public corporate channel.”

90-Second Triage Checklist

  • Who contacted me? (verify independently)
  • What are they asking for? (money/info/action)
  • What happens if I wait 10 minutes and verify?
  • Would I tell a loved one to do this immediately?
