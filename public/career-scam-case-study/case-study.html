Recruiter Impersonation Case Study

This case study documents a common career-scam pattern: fake recruiter outreach, rapid trust-building, unrealistic pay promises, and a fake-check equipment handoff.

What happened

  • Initial contact looked like a normal recruiter follow-up.
  • Interview process was pushed into text-only format.
  • Compensation was disproportionately high for basic remote work.
  • The flow pivoted to “we’ll send a check for equipment.”

Red flags detected

Unexpected recruiter outreach

Out-of-blue outreach can be spoofing.

Text-only interview

Real recruiting normally includes official channels.

Too-good compensation

High pay for low-complexity tasks is a lure.

Check for supplies

Classic fake-check scam mechanics.

Risk ladder

  • Entry risk: Attention hijack + trust priming
  • Mid risk: Data collection (identity and account details)
  • Primary loss: Fake-check financial loss
  • Escalation risk: Identity theft, mule/reshipping exposure

Response protocol

  • Stop contact and preserve screenshots/messages.
  • If money moved, call bank/payment provider immediately.
  • If personal information shared, treat as identity-risk event.
  • Report to FTC, IC3, platform used, and impersonated company.
